## The Axiom

**Shaw is gauged Intent.**

## Shaw in 60 Seconds

Three levels of information exist:

| Level     | Name           | Measures                                      | Receiver-dependent? | Unit?               |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Syntactic | Shannon (1948) | How many bits                                 | No                  | bit                 |
| Semantic  | Meaning        | What does it mean                             | Partially           | None established    |
| Pragmatic | Shaw (2002)    | What difference does it make to THIS receiver | Yes                 | None — and can't be |

Shannon solved the syntactic level and gave it a universal unit (the bit). The bit works because
syntactic information is context-free — a bit is a bit regardless of who receives it.

Shaw (Mårelius & Mellberg, 2002) solved the pragmatic level:

```
IV_i = -log(p_ri)
```

Where p_ri is the probability of receiver **r** expecting to receive Statement **i**.

The **r** subscript makes Shaw explicitly receiver-dependent. Different receivers, different
expectations, different value — for the same Statement. The r IS the gauge parameter, though
the term "gauge" was not used in 2002.

In physics, a **gauge-dependent quantity** changes with the reference frame. Electromagnetic
potential has no "true" value — it depends on where you measure from. Shaw has the same
structure: no "true" value of information — it depends on who receives it.

The structural equivalence is exact:

| Gauge theory property                                  | Shaw property                                     |
|--------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Gauge-dependent quantities change with reference frame | Shaw changes with receiver                        |
| Gauge-invariant quantities are frame-independent       | Shannon bits are receiver-independent             |
| Gauge-fixing = choosing one frame to calculate in      | Intent = choosing one observational frame         |
| Many valid gauges for the same physics                 | Many valid receivers for the same information     |
| No universal description of gauge-dependent quantity   | No universal unit for Shaw                        |
| Physical observables must be gauge-invariant           | Conclusions/decisions should survive gauge change |
| Gauge transformation = change of reference frame       | Receiver change = different Shaw for same signal  |

A universal "Shaw unit" would need to be universal across receivers. But Shaw is gauge-dependent —
it changes with the receiver by definition. A universal unit for a gauge-dependent quantity is a
structural impossibility. The same structural impossibility that prevents a universal value for
electromagnetic potential.

## The Kill Chain — Truth Was Already Dead

Before asking what Shaw does to truth, we should check: was truth alive when Shaw arrived?

**Protagoras (~450 BC)**: "Man is the measure of all things." Truth is relative to the perceiver.
He saw the r subscript 2,400 years before the formula. No mechanism — just the observation.

**Kant (1781)**: We never perceive things-in-themselves (noumenon). We perceive through categories
of understanding — space, time, causality. The categories ARE a gauge. Kant didn't kill truth — he
said it exists but is inaccessible. We only get the gauged version.

**Nietzsche (1887)**: "There are no facts, only interpretations." No universal reference frame.
This IS gauge-dependency stated as philosophy. Nietzsche came closest to killing truth outright.
But he had no mechanism — only the declaration, and the declaration was itself gauge-dependent
(philosophers have been arguing about what he meant ever since).

**Pragmatism — William James (1907)**: Truth is "what works." Truth is instrumentally defined —
what has practical value for the agent. This is remarkably close to Shaw: "true" ≈ "high Shaw for
this receiver." But pragmatism couldn't explain WHY the universal unit can't exist. It simply
observed that truth seemed to be relative to the user and moved on.

**Kuhn (1962)**: Paradigm shifts. Normal science operates within a paradigm. Revolution replaces
it. Scientists in different paradigms are "living in different worlds." Scientific truth is
paradigm-dependent. This IS gauge-dependency of scientific knowledge — but Kuhn contained it to
philosophy of science and didn't connect it to information theory or physics.

**Postmodernism — Derrida (1967), Foucault, Lyotard**: "There is no outside-text." Grand
narratives are dead. Truth is a power structure, not a correspondence to reality. Went further
than Nietzsche — truth is not just interpretation, it's construction. But postmodernism was
dismissed as nihilism and relativism because it lacked formal structure. No math. No predictions.
No mechanism. Just the assertion.

**Quantum mechanics — Heisenberg (1927), Bohr**: No definite state before measurement.
Measurement creates the outcome. This killed truth in physics — there is no "true" position of a
particle before observation. The wavefunction is the complete description; there is nothing "more
real" behind it. But most people treated this as a physics peculiarity — "weird quantum stuff" —
not a statement about truth in general.

**QBism — Fuchs, Schack (~2002)**: Quantum probabilities are subjective — belonging to the agent,
not the system. Quantum states aren't "out there." They're the agent's expectations about future
experience. This IS Shaw's structure applied to physics: the value belongs to the receiver, not to
the message.

**Verdict**: Truth was killed many times, by many people, from many angles. Protagoras wounded it.
Nietzsche declared it dead. Pragmatism offered a replacement. Postmodernism danced on the grave.
Quantum mechanics killed it in physics. Nobody connected these killings.

## Truth Against Shaw's Axioms

If Shaw is gauge-dependent in the physics sense, what happens when we test each meaning of "truth"
against the framework?

### "The facts" — Shannon truth

The bits exist. Shannon information is gauge-invariant — the same regardless of receiver.
A document has the same character count, the same byte sequence, regardless of who reads it.

This is real. But it's syntax without semantics. The bits are there. What they MEAN, what they're
WORTH, what they IMPLY — all gauge-dependent. Shannon truth is the truth of existence: the symbols
are present. That's real, but it's not what anyone means when they say "truth."

### "What really matters" — High Shaw

When someone says "that's true," they often mean "that resonated with me, that matches my model,
that changes how I see." This is high Shaw — high pragmatic value for THIS receiver.

But Shaw is gauge-dependent. What resonates with one receiver is obvious to another. What changes
one person's model is old news to someone else. "E=mc²" has enormous Shaw for a physics student,
near-zero Shaw for Einstein. "What really matters" is always "what really matters TO ME."

### "Real truth" — The intensifier fails

When people add "real" to truth — "the REAL truth" — they mean the most specific, irreducible,
surprising version. The thing nobody wants to hear. The thing that changes everything.

This maps precisely to lowest p_r = highest Shaw. "Real truth" IS "very high Shaw." But Shaw is
gauge-dependent. Even the intensifier fails to escape the gauge. "Real truth" = "very high Shaw
for me" — still receiver-dependent.

### "What everyone agrees on" — BRST aggregation

In quantum field theory, BRST symmetry means: sum over all gauge configurations and the
gauge-dependent parts cancel. Only gauge-invariant structure survives.

Applied to Shaw: survey many receivers. Aggregate their Shaw assessments. Individual biases
cancel. Invariant structure emerges. This is how the Big Five personality factors were discovered —
thousands of gauge-dependent self-reports, factor-analyzed, five invariant dimensions emerge.

This LOOKS like truth. But BRST calls it "gauge-invariant structure," not truth. And BRST fails
under entanglement — when receivers aren't independent (groupthink), individual biases don't
cancel. They amplify. Even aggregation doesn't reliably produce what people call truth. It
produces invariants under independence conditions.

### "What matches reality" — Measurement creates the outcome

The strongest meaning of truth: correspondence to reality. "It's true because it matches what's
really there."

But if cognitive states are quantum — and the evidence suggests they are — measurement creates the
outcome, not reveals it.

**Selective perception** (Hastorf, 1954): Princeton and Dartmouth fans watch the same football
game. Same Shannon — same physical events on the field. Each side sees the other team committing
more fouls. If truth existed as correspondence, there would be ONE game that both are perceiving
imperfectly. But quantum Shaw says: same Shannon, different quantum states, genuinely different
collapsed outcomes. There is no "true game" independent of the observer's state.

**Memory reconstruction** (Loftus, 1974): Witnesses are asked "How fast were the cars going when
they smashed into each other?" versus "...when they hit each other?" Different verb, different
speed estimates — AND different memories. "Smashed" produces false memories of broken glass that
wasn't there. If truth existed, there would be ONE true memory. But the measurement operator
(the question) created the memory. There is no "true" memory independent of how you ask.

These aren't bugs in human cognition. Classical probability predicts they shouldn't happen.
Quantum probability explains them structurally — as features of a system where measurement creates
outcomes, not reveals them.

### "The transformation" — The closest candidate

In quantum field theory, individual field values are gauge-dependent. But the S-matrix — the total
transformation from initial state to final state — is gauge-invariant. It doesn't care about
intermediate dynamics. Only input → output.

Applied to Shaw: the gauge-invariant observable is not "what was the value of message 5?" but
"what did the entire information stream DO to this receiver?" The trajectory, not any point on it.

Two different receivers can undergo structurally equivalent transformations but arrive at different
endpoints. The shape of the change is invariant. The destination is not.

This is the closest thing to "truth" in the framework — but it's not what anyone means by the
word. It's a structural invariant of the transformation process, not a correspondence to reality.

## What the Framework Has Instead

| What people call "truth" | What it is in the framework              | Gauge status                              |
|--------------------------|------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| "The facts"              | Shannon — the bits exist                 | Gauge-invariant, but meaningless (syntax) |
| "What really matters"    | High Shaw — value to THIS receiver       | Gauge-dependent                           |
| "Real truth"             | Very high Shaw                           | Still gauge-dependent                     |
| "What everyone agrees"   | BRST survivors — statistical aggregation | Gauge-invariant under independence        |
| "Matches reality"        | Measurement outcome                      | Created by measurement, not revealed      |
| "The transformation"     | Path integral / S-matrix                 | Gauge-invariant, but not "truth"          |

The framework has Shannon (what exists), Shaw (what matters), Intent (the gauge that determines
Shaw), and BRST (what survives aggregation). None of these is truth. The framework works without
it. Truth is the concept the framework displaces — like "aether" was displaced by special
relativity. Not proven wrong. Made unnecessary.

## What Shaw Changed

Shaw didn't kill truth. Truth was already dead — killed many times, by many people, across 2,400
years. What Shaw changed:

**1. Identified the cause of death.**

Previous truth-killers declared truth dead without explaining why. "There are no facts, only
interpretations" (Nietzsche). "There is no outside-text" (Derrida). Philosophical assertions.
Shaw gives the mechanism: gauge-dependency. The r subscript in IV_i = -log(p_ri). The same
structural impossibility that prevents a universal value for electromagnetic potential prevents a
universal value for pragmatic information. Not a philosophical stance — a mathematical structure.

**2. Connected the witnesses.**

Pragmatism ("truth is what works"), Kuhn ("truth is paradigm-dependent"), postmodernism ("truth is
constructed"), quantum mechanics ("measurement creates the outcome") — all said similar things
from different traditions, in different vocabularies, never connecting their observations. Shaw's
gauge framework shows they're all describing the same structural property. They're not different
philosophical positions. They're different observations of the same mathematical fact.

**3. Turned philosophy into prediction.**

"Truth is relative" — untestable, debatable for 2,400 years. "Shaw is gauge-dependent with SU(n)
gauge group" — testable, falsifiable, with specific hypotheses. If Shaw's gauge structure is real,
it makes predictions about order effects in information processing, about when crowds are wise and
when they aren't, about interference patterns in comprehension. This is what the pragmatists and
postmodernists couldn't do: make the claim scientific.

**4. Explained the absence.**

"Why is there no universal unit of pragmatic information?" Nobody asked this clearly. Shannon
solved the syntactic level in 1948 and gave it the bit. Seventy-six years later, the pragmatic
level remained unsolved. Shaw says: it's not an unsolved problem. It's a structural impossibility.
You cannot have a universal unit for a gauge-dependent quantity. The absence IS the finding.

## The Recursive Close

This document is itself a demonstration.

The claim "Shaw implies truth is gauge-dependent" is a statement with Shaw value. That value is
gauge-dependent:

- To a philosopher familiar with Nietzsche and pragmatism: moderate Shaw. They've heard this
  before, in different words. The new element is the formalism.
- To a physicist: high Shaw. The gauge structure is immediately visible. The connection to their
  field is novel.
- To someone without either background: zero Shaw. They see a formula with a subscript.

Same Shannon content. Different Shaw. No "true" reading of this document. The document
demonstrates itself recursively — it is a gauge-delivery device that hands the reader a gauge
through which truth's structural impossibility becomes visible.

Before the gauge: "truth is problematic" — a vague philosophical discomfort.
After the gauge: "truth is gauge-dependent" — a structural fact with the same status as "electromagnetic potential is
gauge-dependent."

Shaw didn't kill truth. Shaw explained why it was never alive.
